Planet Size

Planet size dictates the size and resolution of the simulation. More complex shapes are possible with a larger map and larger plant and animal populations are possible. The `end game` of a simulation can be achieved faster on a smaller map, and smaller maps and populations will generally simulate faster, allowing you to see the results faster.

Planet Rotation Direction

Planet rotation dictates which direction the rainfall flows. For instance, Earth's West to East rotation means that its greater wind currents flow East to West. Continental geography in the East cuts off warm currents from reaching the West. The Himilayan Mountains cut off warm equatorial currents from reaching Central Asia and the Ethiopian Mountains cut off rainfall to the Sahara.

Planet Tilt

Planet tilt dictates which hemisphere recieves the most sun. For instance, on Earth, the 23.5 degree tilt means that the sourthern hemisphere recieves the most sunlight annually. More sunlight allows for denser foliage and greater rainfall, meaning this `Sunlight Equator` will generally have a belt of rainforest.
